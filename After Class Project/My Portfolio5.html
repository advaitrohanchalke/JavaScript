Chinese Cuisines

Chinese cuisine is traditionally defined by the Four Great Traditions, each offering a distinct flavor profile: Sichuan cuisine is world-famous for its bold, fiery, and numbing spices; Cantonese cuisine focuses on mild, delicate seasoning to preserve the natural freshness of its ingredients; Shandong cuisine specializes in savory seafood and rich, complex broths; and Jiangsu cuisine emphasizes meticulous, artistic presentation with a tender balance of sweet and savory notes.
